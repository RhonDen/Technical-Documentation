Introduction

League of Legends is a popular multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game where players assume various roles to achieve victory. Each role has its unique responsibilities and playstyles, contributing to the teamâ€™s success.

Top Lane

The Top Lane is typically home to tanky, durable champions who excel in 1v1 duels and sustain damage for their team. They often focus on split-pushing and flanking enemies in team fights.

Example Champion: Darius, Malphite
  • Position: Solo
  • Playstyle: Tank, Bruiser
Jungle

The Jungler is responsible for controlling neutral objectives like Dragons and Barons. They also roam the map to gank lanes and provide vision control for their team.

Example Champion: Lee Sin, Amumu
  • Position: Roamer
  • Playstyle: Assassin, Tank
Mid Lane

The Mid Laner often plays mages or assassins who are key in dealing high amounts of damage to the enemy. This role is central to a team's strategy, controlling the middle of the map.

Example Champion: Ahri, Zed
  • Position: Solo
  • Playstyle: Mage, Assassin
ADC (Attack Damage Carry)

The ADC is the primary damage dealer in the team, focusing on auto-attacks and building items that enhance their attack power. They usually stay in the bot lane with a support champion.

Example Champion: Jinx, Kai'Sa
  • Position: Duo with Support
  • Playstyle: Marksman
Support

The Support protects the ADC and provides utility to the team with healing, shields, or crowd control. They focus on vision control and enabling their team to succeed in fights.

Example Champion: Thresh, Lulu
  • Position: Duo with ADC
  • Playstyle: Enchanter, Tank
